Your roof takes a battering every year — wind, rain, frost, and UV all slowly wear it down. The good news is that most serious problems start small, and if you catch them early you can save yourself a significant amount of money.

Here's what to look out for.

## Missing or Lifted Tiles

After any storm, take a walk around the outside of your house and look up. Missing tiles are obvious, but lifted or slightly raised tiles are just as dangerous — they let wind and water get underneath and cause damage you can't see from the ground.

## Damp Patches on Ceilings

If you notice a yellowish stain appearing on an upstairs ceiling, that's almost always water coming from above. Don't ignore it hoping it'll dry out — by the time it's visible inside, water has usually been sitting on the roof for a while.

## Blocked or Sagging Gutters

Gutters that are full of moss and debris can't drain properly. Water backs up and sits against the roof edge, rotting the fascia boards underneath. Check your gutters twice a year — spring and autumn — and clear them out.

## Moss and Algae Growth

A little moss isn't the end of the world, but thick growth holds moisture against your tiles and speeds up deterioration. It's also a sign your roof isn't draining properly. Treatment is straightforward and much cheaper than replacing tiles.

## Flashing Problems

Flashing is the metal strip that seals joins around chimneys, skylights and where the roof meets a wall. When it lifts or cracks, water gets straight in. It's one of the most common causes of leaks and one of the easiest fixes if caught early.

## What to Do

If you spot any of these signs, don't leave it until winter. A quick inspection now could save you thousands down the line.
